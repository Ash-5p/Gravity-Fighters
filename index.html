Who are we?

Gravity Fighters is a club, based in Stoke-On-Trent, open to everyone who is interested in getting into weightlifting.

Whether you're looking to get big and strong, fit and lean, or you're just looking for a fun and rewarding hobby, Gravity Fighters can offer the support you need on your fitness journey.

Types of Weightlifting

If you've ever entered a gym in mid-January, it would be easy to think that weightlifting is all bicep curls and crunches, but what if I told you there were many styles of weighlifting suited to many different goals. We at Gravity Fighters love and embrace all facets of weightlifting and encourage everyone, beginner lifter or advanced, to give each one a try!

Bodybuilding

Bodybuilding is what most people think of when you mention weightlifting. The sport was made popular by the likes of Arnold Schwarzenegger & Ronnie Coleman. As the name suggests, the goal is to build a large muscular physique, and is more focused on asthetics than overall strength. To learn more about bodybuilding click here.

Powerlifting

Powerlifting focuses on 3 key lifts: Squat, Bench Press & Deadlift, all using a barbell. The usual format for a powerlifting competitition requires the competitor to perform 1 rep of each lift at maximum weight, typically getting 3 attemps at each lift. This is an exellent format for those who want to chase those strength goals. To learn more about powerlifting click here.

Olympic Weightlifting

Olympic weightlifting is a competetive format which athletes attempt to lift a weighted barbell above their heads using one of 2 techniques: Snatch or Clean & Jerk. This type of weightlifting is not only a great way to build strength, but also mobility and coordination, due to the technique required to execute these type of lifts effectively. To learn more about olympic weightlifting click here.
